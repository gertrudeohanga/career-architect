#### Q6: "How do you handle rejection in sales?"

**Response**:

"I view rejection as part of the business development process, not personal failure. In sales, you hear 'no' far more often than 'yes' - it's about the numbers and persistence.

**Mindset**: At Ngao Credit, I prospected 200+ leads knowing many would say no. I maintained resilience by focusing on the conversion rate - if I make 100 calls and convert 10, that's 10 wins. I stay positive, learn from each interaction, and move to the next prospect quickly.

**Learning Approach**: I analyze rejections to understand why - was it timing, pricing, fit, or my approach? This helps me refine my strategy. When I improved my qualification process at My-Credit, I increased conversion rates by 25% by better identifying qualified prospects upfront.

**Persistence**: I don't take the first 'no' as final. I maintain relationships with prospects who aren't ready now - they might be ready later. At Schmsoft, 40% of my business came from prospects who initially said no but converted after ongoing relationship nurturing.

**Results Focus**: I stay motivated by tracking my wins and celebrating progress. When I exceeded targets by 30%+ at Ngao Credit, it was because I maintained high activity levels despite daily rejections. The wins outweighed the losses."

   - Demonstrates strategic thinking

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **Unrealistic expectations**: Expecting immediate healthcare expertise without training
- **No training/support**: Not willing to invest in your healthcare education
- **Unclear success metrics**: Can't articulate what success looks like
- **High turnover**: Previous BD officers didn't last long
- **Resistance to fresh ideas**: "We've always done it this way" mentality
- **Compensation concerns**: Below-market salary without growth potential
