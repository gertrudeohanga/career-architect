# Interview Preparation

## Company Research
- **Name:** Pesapal
- **Industry:** Payment Service Provider (Fintech)
- **Product Focus:** Oracle Hospitality Solutions (Micros, Opera, Simphony).
- **Goal:** Pesapal is an Oracle partner; they sell/integrate these systems for hotels/restaurants.
- **Why it matters:** Hotels need these systems to manage bookings (PMS) and food/beverage (POS).

## Key Competencies
1.  **Solution Selling:** Moving away from "product features" to "business value" (ROI, efficiency).
2.  **Hospitality Knowledge:** Understanding hotel ops (Front desk, F&B, Back office).
3.  **Tech Fluency:** Understanding Cloud, SaaS, Integrations.

## Likely Interview Questions

### Sales Process
**Q: "Walk me through a complex sale you closed."**
*Answer:* Focus on the *Kwendo* experience.
- **Discovery:** "I met with the CFO to understand their cash flow gaps..."
- **Solution:** "I proposed a solution that automated their reconciliations..."
- **Stakeholders:** "I had to get buy-in from IT regarding security..."
- **Close:** "We negotiated terms and I secured the contract."

**Q: "How do you build a pipeline?"**
*Answer:* "I am disciplined with my CRM.
1.  **Prospecting:** LinkedIn, Industry events, Referrals.
2.  **Qualification:** BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline).
3.  **Nurturing:** Regular follow-ups, sharing industry insights.
4.  **Closing:** Moving them through the stages efficiently."

### Technical/Product
**Q: "What do you know about Oracle Hospitality?"**
*Answer:* "Oracle is the gold standard for hotels (Opera) and restaurants (Micros/Simphony). It's not just software; it's the nervous system of a hotel. It manages reservations, inventory, reporting, and guest experience. Selling it means helping a hotel GM run a better, more profitable business."

**Q: "You don't have Oracle experience. How will you cope?"**
*Answer:* "I am a fast learner and I understand business systems. At Kwendo/Tenakata, I had to learn financial platforms quickly. The principles of ERP and Enterprise systems—integrating data, improving workflow, ensuring security—are universal. I will dedicate my first 30 days to mastering the product suite."
