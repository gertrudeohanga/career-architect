## Technical/Role-Specific Questions

### Q: "How do you prioritize when you have multiple demo requests, follow-ups, and agreement processing all due simultaneously?"

**Answer**: I use a prioritization matrix based on: (1) deal size and likelihood to close, (2) client urgency and business impact, (3) dependencies (e.g., agreements blocking implementation), and (4) time sensitivity. I communicate transparently with clients about timelines and set clear expectations. For example, at Kwendo, I would prioritize a demo for a high-value prospect ready to make a decision over routine follow-up with a long-term prospect, while ensuring nothing was neglected through disciplined calendar blocking and CRM task management.

### Q: "What CRM tools have you used, and how do you ensure data quality?"

**Answer**: I have worked with various CRM platforms including [specific systems from master experience]. My approach to data quality includes: (1) updating CRM immediately after every client interaction while details are fresh, (2) standardized note formats for consistency, (3) regular data hygiene reviews, and (4) using CRM insights to inform strategy. Accurate data is critical for forecasting, pipeline management, and personalized client engagement.

### Q: "How do you handle rejection or a demo that doesn't convert?"

**Answer**: I view every non-conversion as a learning opportunity. My process: (1) conduct a brief self-assessment of the demo to identify improvement areas, (2) seek feedback from the prospect if possible, (3) update CRM with detailed notes about why they didn't convert (timing, features, budget), (4) schedule appropriate follow-up timing for future re-engagement, and (5) share learnings with the team if patterns emerge. Resilience and continuous improvement are essential in sales.

### Q: "How would you explain a complex technical product to a non-technical buyer?"

**Answer**: I use the "so what" framework: (1) start with their business problem, not the product features, (2) explain the solution in plain language using analogies if helpful, (3) demonstrate tangible outcomes and ROI, (4) use visuals or simple examples rather than technical jargon, and (5) check for understanding and invite questions. At Kwendo, I regularly explained payroll digitization to HR managers without technical backgrounds by focusing on outcomes—eliminating ghost workers, reducing fraud, improving efficiency—rather than technical architecture.
