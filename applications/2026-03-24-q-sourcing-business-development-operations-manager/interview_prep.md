### Q5: How would you manage a situation where a client's travel plans are disrupted (flight cancellation, hotel overbooking, etc.)?

**Why they ask**: Testing problem-solving, customer service, and operations management under pressure

**Your answer**:
"Crisis management in travel requires calm, swift action, and client-first mentality:

**Immediate Response**:
1. **Acknowledge & Empathize**: Contact client immediately, acknowledge the disruption, express understanding of their frustration
2. **Assess Options**: Quickly identify alternative solutions (alternative flights, hotels, itinerary adjustments)
3. **Present Solutions**: Offer 2-3 options with pros/cons, let client choose based on their priorities
4. **Execute Swiftly**: Implement chosen solution immediately, confirm all bookings, update itinerary
5. **Compensate**: Offer compensation where appropriate (upgrades, discounts, added services)

**Follow-Through**:
- Monitor situation to ensure smooth continuation
- Maintain communication throughout
- Follow up post-trip to ensure satisfaction
- Document lessons learned to prevent future issues

**Evidence**: While I haven't handled travel disruptions specifically, I've managed client issues in high-pressure situations. At Ngao Credit, when loan approvals were delayed, I proactively communicated with clients, provided alternatives, and maintained relationships despite challenges. At Kwendo, when platform issues affected clients, I coordinated with technical teams for swift resolution while keeping clients informed.

The principles are the same: acknowledge the problem, act swiftly, communicate transparently, prioritize client needs, and turn a negative into an opportunity to demonstrate exceptional service."
