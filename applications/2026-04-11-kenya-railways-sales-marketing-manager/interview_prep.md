## Technical/Role-Specific Questions

### Q1: How would you formulate and implement a sales and marketing strategy for Kenya Railways' freight services to increase business volumes?

**Why they ask**: Testing strategic thinking, understanding of railway commercial services, and ability to develop actionable plans

**Key points to cover**:
- Market research and competitive analysis
- Stakeholder identification (shipping lines, clearing agents, logistics companies)
- Partnership development strategy
- Pricing strategy and value proposition
- Customer acquisition and retention plans
- Performance metrics and monitoring

**Your evidence**: At Kwendo, developed and implemented sales strategies achieving 25% quarter-over-quarter client acquisition growth; at My-Credit, drove strategies achieving 120% of annual sales targets

**Model answer outline**:
- **Situation**: "In my role at Kwendo, I faced a similar challenge of penetrating a market with established players..."
- **Action**: "I conducted comprehensive market research to identify key decision-makers, developed targeted value propositions, built strategic partnerships with complementary service providers, and implemented a phased rollout strategy..."
- **Result**: "This approach resulted in 25% quarter-over-quarter growth and positioned us as market leaders in our segment"
- **Application**: "For Kenya Railways, I would apply this same systematic approach: first conducting stakeholder mapping of shipping lines and logistics companies, then developing tailored value propositions for freight services, establishing strategic partnerships, and implementing performance tracking systems"

**Pitfalls to avoid**:
- Generic answers without specific strategies
- Lack of understanding of railway/logistics industry
- No mention of stakeholder engagement
- Missing performance metrics
