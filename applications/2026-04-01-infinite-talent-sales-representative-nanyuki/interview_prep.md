## Technical/Skills Questions

### Q1: Walk me through your typical sales process from prospecting to closing.

**Why they ask**: Testing sales methodology and systematic thinking

**Key points**: Demonstrate structured approach, relationship focus, needs-based selling

**Your evidence**: Ngao Credit sales process; My-Credit client acquisition

**Model answer outline**:
1. **Prospecting**: Identify potential clients through networks, referrals, community engagement, digital platforms
2. **Qualification**: Assess fit—do they have the need? Can they afford it? Are they decision-makers?
3. **Needs Assessment**: Ask questions to understand their specific situation and pain points
4. **Solution Presentation**: Present product/service as solution to their specific needs (not generic pitch)
5. **Objection Handling**: Address concerns transparently and honestly
6. **Closing**: Ask for the commitment; make next steps clear
7. **Follow-up**: Ensure smooth onboarding/delivery; maintain relationship for repeat business and referrals

**Pitfalls**: Don't make it sound transactional; emphasize relationship building throughout

---

### Q2: How do you track your sales performance and pipeline?

**Why they ask**: Testing organizational skills and data-driven approach

**Key points**: Show systematic approach, use of tools, proactive tracking

**Your evidence**: Maintained accurate CRM records at Ngao Credit; tracked performance metrics at Tenakata

**Model answer outline**:
- Use CRM or tracking system (Excel/Google Sheets if no CRM)
- Track key metrics: number of prospects, conversion rates, average deal size, time to close
- Categorize pipeline by stage (prospect, qualified, proposal, negotiation, closed)
- Set weekly activity goals (calls, meetings, proposals) not just outcome goals

**Pitfalls**: Don't claim expertise with tools you haven't used; focus on the systematic approach
