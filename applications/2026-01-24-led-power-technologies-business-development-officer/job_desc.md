## Job Purpose

The Business Development Officer is responsible for identifying, pursuing, and managing business opportunities, particularly in tendering and government contracts. This role bridges commercial strategy with operational execution, ensuring LED Power Technologies wins high-value tenders and maintains a healthy project pipeline.

## Key Responsibilities

1. **Tender Identification & Pipeline Management**
   - Identify and research tender opportunities across government, private, and development sectors
   - Build and maintain a comprehensive tender pipeline
   - Track tender deadlines, requirements, and bid preparation timelines
   - Maintain quarterly pipeline growth targets

2. **Tender Response & Bid Management**
   - Prepare comprehensive tender responses and bid documents
   - Coordinate with technical and operational teams to develop competitive bids
   - Ensure all submissions meet compliance requirements and deadlines
   - Manage bid documentation, pricing, and commercial positioning

3. **Relationship Development**
   - Build and maintain relationships with government procurement officers
   - Engage with development partners (World Bank, AfDB, bilateral donors)
   - Network with procurement contacts at large private organizations
   - Attend tender pre-bid meetings and conferences

4. **Commercial Positioning**
   - Develop competitive pricing strategies
   - Analyze competitor bids and market positioning
   - Identify gaps in LED Power's service offerings
   - Propose commercial positioning for different tender types

5. **Compliance & Quality Assurance**
   - Ensure all submissions are compliant with tender requirements
   - Verify document completeness (zero disqualifications)
   - Maintain submission schedules (100% on-time submission)
   - Implement quality control processes for tender documents

6. **Performance Tracking & Reporting**
   - Report tender success rates and pipeline metrics
   - Analyze bid win/loss reasons
   - Prepare monthly business development reports
   - Track KPIs and identify improvement areas

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

1. **Pipeline Growth**: Maintain quarterly increase in tender pipeline value
2. **Submission Timeliness**: 100% on-time submission rate (submissions delivered 24 hours before deadline minimum)
3. **Tender Success Rate**: Target bid win rate percentage (typically 15-25% in construction tendering)
4. **Disqualification Rate**: Zero administrative disqualifications (missing documents, compliance failures)
5. **Relationship Development**: Number of new government/private sector relationships established monthly
6. **Proposal Quality**: Accuracy and completeness metrics for bid submissions
