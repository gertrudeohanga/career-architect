## Core Interview Themes

### 1. Distributor Management
**Question:** "How do you manage a distributor who is underperforming?"
**Answer:** "I use a data-driven approach. First, I analyze the gap: Is it **Stock** (Availability), **Sales Team** (Capability), or **Finance** (Working Capital)?
- If **Stock:** We review order cycles.
- If **Team:** I join them for a market visit to coach.
- If **Finance:** We review the ROI and credit terms.
I believe in 'Joint Business Planning'â€”getting buy-in on the fix, not just dictating it."

### 2. Territory Strategy / Route to Market (RTM)
**Question:** "You are given a new territory. How do you plan to grow it?"
**Answer:** "I start with **Mapping**. Currently, what is our Numeric Distribution (how many shops have stock)?
1.  **Segmentation:** Tier the outlets (Gold, Silver, Bronze).
2.  **Coverage:** Ensure the sales team's journey plan covers the high-potential outlets efficiently.
3.  **Gap Analysis:** Identify 'White Spaces' where we have zero presence and assign a 'Hunter' strategy to recruit new stockists there."

### 3. Team Leadership
**Question:** "Tell me about a time you motivated a demotivated team."
**Answer:** "At My-Credit, morale dipped after a target hike. I organized a 'Field Blitz' week where I went out with the team every day. We created daily mini-incentives (airtime/lunch) for 'Fastest Start' or 'Most Visits'. Seeing me in the trenches with them changed the energy. We hit the weekly target, but more importantly, belief was restored."

### 4. Financial Hygiene
**Question:** "Why is financial hygiene important in sales?"
**Answer:** "Sales without hygiene is just 'Vanity'. Unreconciled accounts lead to disputes, which lead to partners stopping orders. I prioritize weekly reconciliations and strict credit limits to ensure the 'pipe' remains unblocked. A healthy distributor is a selling distributor."
