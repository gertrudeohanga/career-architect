### Q1: "Tell me about a time when you had to build a relationship with a difficult client and turn them into a satisfied customer."

**Why they ask**: Testing relationship management, persistence, and problem-solving

**Your evidence**: Ngao Credit - Full-cycle sales with hands-on client support

**SAR Answer**:

**Situation**: "At Ngao Credit, I worked with a client who was initially very skeptical about logbook loans due to a negative experience with another provider. He was defensive, asked numerous challenging questions, and seemed ready to walk away from our first meeting."

**Action**: "Rather than pushing for the sale, I took a consultative approach. I spent extra time understanding his previous experience and specific concerns about interest rates, repayment flexibility, and vehicle security. I provided complete transparency about our terms, walked him through every document in detail, and connected him with existing satisfied clients who could share their experiences. I also followed up personally at each stage of the application process, proactively addressing concerns before he had to ask."

**Result**: "Not only did he proceed with the loan, but he became one of my strongest advocates, referring 8 additional clients over the following year. This experience reinforced my belief that investing time in building trust and providing exceptional service converts skeptics into loyal clients. It contributed to my 60% repeat business rate and 40+ annual referrals at Ngao."

## Weak Point Defense

### Potential Concern #1: Lack of Direct Recruitment Industry Experience

**Likely question**: "You have strong business development experience, but you haven't worked in recruitment. Why should we hire you over someone with recruitment industry experience?"

**Your pivot**: 
"That's a fair question, and I'd argue that my diverse industry experience is actually an advantage. The core competencies of business development—understanding client needs, building trust, matching solutions to requirements, and driving revenue growth—are universal. What changes is the product, not the process. At Kwendo, I quickly became effective in digital payroll and HR-tech despite coming from microfinance because I focused on mastering the fundamentals: understanding client pain points, articulating value propositions, and building relationships. In fact, my outside perspective often helps me see opportunities that industry insiders might miss because they're too close to conventional approaches. I'm a quick learner—I became a subject matter expert in payroll digitization within a month at Kwendo—and I'm confident I can do the same with recruitment. What I bring is proven BD success, fresh perspectives, and transferable skills that will deliver results from day one."

### Potential Concern #2: Salary Expectations vs. Offered Range

**Likely question**: "What are your salary expectations for this role?"

**Your pivot**:
"I'm looking for a role where I can make a significant impact and grow professionally. While compensation is important, I'm more focused on the opportunity to contribute to Studio Twenty Two's growth and develop my career in the recruitment industry. Based on my research of the market and my 5+ years of senior-level business development experience with a consistent track record of exceeding targets, I believe my value justifies compensation in the range of KSh 80,000-100,000. However, I'm open to discussing a total compensation package that includes performance bonuses, commission structures, or other benefits that align my success with the company's success. I'm confident that once I demonstrate my ability to drive revenue growth and client acquisition, we'll both see this as a valuable investment. What's the compensation structure for this role, and is there flexibility based on performance?"

**Alternative approach if they push back**:
"I understand the stated range is KSh 45,000-60,000. Given my senior-level experience and proven track record, I'd like to propose a performance-based structure: a base salary within your range plus aggressive commission or bonus tied to specific metrics like new client acquisition, revenue growth, or client retention. This way, my compensation directly reflects the value I deliver, and you minimize risk while I have unlimited upside potential. Would that structure be possible?"

### Potential Concern #3: Multiple Short-Term Roles

**Likely question**: "I notice you've had several roles in the past few years. Can you explain the frequent moves?"

**Your pivot**:
"Great observation. Each move was strategic and driven by growth opportunities. Tenakata was a short-term contract role (June-August 2025) that I took while transitioning to Kwendo, where I saw the opportunity to work with cutting-edge HR-tech and expand my experience in digital platforms. Before that, my role at Ngao (June 2024-June 2025) was a full year where I consistently exceeded targets, and I left because I wanted to move into a more strategic BD role rather than transactional sales. My two years at My-Credit (May 2022-May 2024) demonstrated stability and progressive growth. What's consistent across all these roles is my track record of delivering results—exceeding targets, building strong client relationships, and driving growth. I'm now looking for a long-term opportunity where I can build something substantial, and Studio Twenty Two's growth trajectory and the recruitment industry's potential make this an exciting place to invest my career."

## Final Tips

### During the Interview

- **Energy & Enthusiasm**: Show genuine excitement about the opportunity
- **Concrete Examples**: Always back up claims with specific stories and metrics
- **Ask Clarifying Questions**: If you don't understand a question, ask for clarification
- **Take Brief Notes**: Shows you're engaged and helps you remember details
- **Mirror Their Energy**: Match their communication style (formal vs. conversational)
- **Use Their Language**: Incorporate terminology they use when describing the role

### Body Language (In-Person)

- Firm handshake
- Good eye contact (but not staring)
- Sit up straight, lean slightly forward (shows engagement)
- Smile naturally
- Avoid fidgeting or crossing arms

### Virtual Interview Specific

- Look at the camera when speaking (not the screen)
- Ensure good lighting (face should be well-lit)
- Plain, professional background
- Frame yourself properly (head and shoulders visible)
- Minimize distractions in your environment

### After the Interview

- Send a thank-you email within 24 hours
- Reference specific topics discussed
- Reiterate your interest and key qualifications
- Keep it brief (3-4 paragraphs)
