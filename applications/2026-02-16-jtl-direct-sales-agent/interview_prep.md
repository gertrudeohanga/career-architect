## 2. Advanced Sales Scenarios

### Scenario: "Sell me Faiba Home 90Mbps vs Safaricom 50Mbps."
**Answer:** "Sir/Madam, Safaricom is a great company, but they sell 'Shared' connections. During peak hours (7pm), your 50Mbps could drop to 10Mbps because everyone in your building is watching Netflix. **Faiba 90Mbps is Dedicated.** You get what you pay for, all the time. Plus, we give you 4G data backup. If a construction worker accidentally cuts the fiber line outside, your internet doesn't drop—it switches to our 4G towers automatically. Can Safaricom do that?"

### Scenario: Handling "It's too expensive" (Price Objection)
**Strategy:** Pivot to **Opportunity Cost**.
**Answer:** "I understand price is a concern. But let me ask—do you work from home? If your internet drops during an important meeting, what does that cost you in professional reputation or missed opportunities? Faiba isn't the cheapest, it's the most *reliable*. We are the insurance policy for your digital life."

## 3. Behavioral Questions (SAR Method)

### Q: "Tell me about a time you faced extreme rejection."
- **Situation:** At Ngao Credit, selling logbook loans in a flooded market.
- **Action:** I didn't stop at the 10th 'No'. I realized my approach was too 'salesy'. I shifted to 'Consultative'. Instead of saying 'Give me your logbook', I asked 'How could an extra 500k help your business expand this month?'
- **Result:** Converted 3 previously 'dead' leads into KES 1.5M in disbursements that week.
- **Key Takeaway:** Rejection is just feedback that your current pitch isn't hitting the right pain point.

### Q: "How do you handle high targets and pressure?"
- **Situation:** Consistently tasked with >KES 2M monthly targets at Ngao and My-Credit.
- **Action:** I break the target down to the **Daily Activity level**. To hit 2M, I need 4 sign-ups. To get 4 sign-ups, I need 20 site visits. To get 20 visits, I need 50 cold calls.
- **Result:** Consistently surpassed quotas by >120% because I focus on the *activity* I can control, rather than just the number.

## 4. Territory Mastery (The Day 1 Plan)
**Question:** "How will you win in a new territory like Westlands?"

1.  **Morning (8am - 11am): B2B Attack.** Visit small businesses, salons, and law firms. They need stability for payments and client research. Sell *Faiba Business*.
2.  **Mid-day (11am - 2pm): Gatekeeper Relationship Building.** Talk to caretakers and security guards. They know exactly who is complaining about their current ISP. Use small incentives (airtime/referral talk) to get the "intelligence".
3.  **Late Afternoon (4pm - 7pm): FTTH Conversion.** Position yourself at the entrance of high-end apartments as residents return. Use a "Neighborhood Promo" hook.
