# Interview Preparation - Mhasibu Sacco

## Company Research

**About Mhasibu Sacco**:
- Type: Savings and Credit Cooperative Society (SACCO)
- Location: Nairobi, Kenya
- Focus: Member-owned financial cooperative providing savings and credit services

**SACCO Sector Context**:
- SACCOs are member-owned financial cooperatives
- Governed by SACCO Societies Act and regulated by SASRA (SACCO Societies Regulatory Authority)
- Focus on financial inclusion and member empowerment
- Democratic governance (one member, one vote)
- Provide savings, credit, and other financial services to members
- Play critical role in Kenya's financial sector

**Key SACCO Values**:
- Member ownership and democratic control
- Member economic participation
- Autonomy and independence
- Education, training and information
- Cooperation among cooperatives
- Concern for community

### Product Knowledge & Presentation

**Q3: How do you present and promote financial products to prospective members?**

**A**: I believe in consultative selling - understanding needs before presenting solutions. I start by asking questions to understand the prospect's financial situation, goals, and challenges. Then I present products that genuinely address their needs, using clear language and real examples they can relate to. At My-Credit, I conducted group presentations at community meetings, using stories of how other members benefited from our services. I also address concerns upfront - if someone is worried about interest rates or repayment terms, I explain clearly and honestly. For SACCOs, I would emphasize the member ownership aspect - you're not just a customer, you're an owner sharing in the Society's success.

---

**Q4: What do you know about SACCO products and services?**

**A**: SACCOs typically offer savings products (regular savings, fixed deposits, holiday savings), credit products (emergency loans, development loans, school fees loans), and increasingly digital services like mobile banking. The key difference from banks is member ownership - members are both customers and owners, sharing in profits through dividends. SACCOs also often offer better rates than banks because they're not-for-profit and focused on member benefit. While I haven't worked specifically in a SACCO, my microfinance experience with similar products (savings, credit, group lending) transfers directly. I'm excited to learn the specific products Mhasibu Sacco offers and how they benefit members.
