- **FOSA (Front Office Service Activity):** Day-to-day banking services

### 2. Role-Specific Questions

**Q: How would you cross-sell SACCO products to existing members?**

*Sample Answer:*
> "Cross-selling starts with understanding each member's complete financial picture. When I meet with a member, I ask about their goals—are they saving for education, building a business, planning for retirement? Then I map SACCO products to those needs. For instance, a member with a savings account might benefit from a fixed deposit for higher returns, or a loan product if they're expanding a business. At Schmsoft Ventures, I increased product uptake by 40% by training myself on all product features and proactively recommending solutions during every client interaction. I'd apply the same approach at QONA SACCO—every member touchpoint is an opportunity to add value through relevant product recommendations."

---

**Q: How would you drive loan penetration?**

*Sample Answer:*
> "Loan penetration requires both demand creation and process efficiency. To create demand, I'd educate members on loan benefits through workshops, one-on-one meetings, and marketing campaigns. At My-Credit, I drove loan applications by simplifying the process—I'd pre-qualify members during recruitment, help them prepare documentation, and guide them through application. For penetration specifically, I'd analyze the member database to identify members who have savings but haven't taken loans, then target them with personalized outreach explaining how a loan could help them achieve their goals. I'd also build relationships with group leaders who can influence their members' borrowing decisions."

### 3. Behavioral Questions

**Q: Tell us about a time you exceeded your sales targets. What made the difference?**

*Sample Answer (STAR Format):*
> **Situation:** At My-Credit Limited, I was assigned to expand our presence in a new market area where we had minimal recognition.
>
> **Task:** I needed to recruit new client groups and achieve loan disbursement targets in an area with established competitors.
>
> **Action:** I researched the area to understand the local business landscape and identified women's groups as an underserved segment. I attended village meetings, built relationships with group leaders, and offered to conduct free financial literacy training. I registered 15 new groups in three months, ensuring each member understood our products. I followed up weekly, helped with documentation, and became their trusted advisor.
>
> **Result:** I achieved 120% of my annual target, with the new groups contributing significantly to portfolio growth. Many became referral sources, creating a sustainable pipeline for future recruitment.

**Q: How do you handle rejection from potential members/clients?**

*Sample Answer:*
> "Rejection is part of sales—I never take it personally. When a prospect says no, I try to understand why. Is it timing? Wrong product? Competitor loyalty? This information helps me either address their concern or know when to follow up later. At Ngao Credit, I kept a 'not now' list of prospects who weren't ready but showed interest. I'd check in every few months with market updates or new product information. About 20% of my sales eventually came from this nurture list. The key is to leave every interaction positively so the door stays open for future engagement."
